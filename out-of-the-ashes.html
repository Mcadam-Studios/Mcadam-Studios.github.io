Out of the Ashes

A grounded post-collapse story universe about family, leadership under pressure, and the brutal maths of survival — where the most dangerous thing isn’t the wasteland, it’s what people become when the rules vanish.

Buy Book 1 on Amazon Back to Worlds

Logline

After government greed and human negligence fracture society, a father and his two children fight through a collapsing Britain toward the promised safety of the Green Zone — but survival demands choices that threaten to break the family long before the world does.

Grounded / Character-led

What It Feels Like

Real roads. Real hunger. Real fear. The apocalypse isn’t a fireworks show — it’s quiet systems failing, one service at a time, until the only law left is the one you can enforce. The tone stays human: tense, emotional, occasionally darkly funny.

Tension + humanity

Core Themes

• Leadership under pressure
• Family as the last institution
• Hope with a price tag
• Moral trade-offs and consequence
• The danger of “safe zones” and false promises

Theme-first storytelling

The Hook

Out of the Ashes isn’t about zombies or spectacle — it’s about what collapses inside people when the outside world collapses. The biggest threat is rarely a monster. It’s a desperate human with a plan.

No gimmicks

Main Characters

Callum — a fiercely devoted father trying to keep his children alive, even when it costs him his own humanity.

Lucy (7) — brave, fierce, and stubbornly hopeful; a daddy’s girl who refuses to be treated like a passenger.

Thomas (4) — younger, vulnerable, and the emotional anchor; his safety is the line that can’t be crossed.

Character-led stakes

The Story Engine

Each episode/chapter forces a choice: food vs morality, speed vs safety, truth vs cohesion. The family’s decisions ripple outward, creating allies, enemies, and consequences that follow them.

Choice → consequence

Signature Tension

A safe place can be worse than the road. A leader can be right and still lose the group. A “good choice” can cost too much. The drama comes from pressure, not plot tricks.

Pressure cooker

Roadmap

Book 1: Ash and Embers — survival on the road; the family’s bond is tested; the Green Zone becomes the North Star.

Book 2: Children of the Collapse — the world expands; leadership fractures; new factions form as resources vanish.

Prequel: Flags in the Fire — the political and social fuse that lit the collapse; the revolution begins.

Built to scale

Season 1 Pitch (TV Adaptation Ready)

Season 1 tracks the family’s journey toward the Green Zone. The world collapses in layers — supply chains, policing, medical care — while the family meets survivors who reflect different responses to collapse: denial, predation, community, fanaticism. The season ends with arrival at the Green Zone… and the unsettling question: safe for who?
